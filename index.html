3D LOPA Development

Mechanical Design Intern - TEAGUE

3D LOPA Development - TEAGUE

LOPA, or "Layout of Passenger Accommodations" is a document outlining the configuration of an aircraft, from the location of galleys to the number of rows of seats and location of exit doors. To better visualize some preliminary airline LOPAs, 3D LOPAs were developed. These models, designed in Rhino 5, showed locations of doors and architecture configurations throughout aircraft sections, and were used by industrial design to better visualize concepts in the scale of the full aircraft. I worked to rebuild these LOPAs from the ground up natively in CATIA V5 for mechanical design use.

Haptic Feedback Prototyping

Mechanical Design Intern - TEAGUE

Haptic Feedback Prototyping - TEAGUE

The linear haptic device was an interesting project, as it had only been developed on the whiteboard. No physical or digital concepts existed. The project was purely up to my imagination, with a goal to design and produce revision one by the end of the summer. The linear haptic device in its purest form is a customizable module to be placed in aircraft mockups. It actuates a force which mimics latches, buttons and more, all customized to a clients needs. Designed in CATIA V5 with touchups and renderings in SolidWorks.

Cooling Ducts

Formula SAE - University of Washington

Formula SAE Team 25

The cooling ducting is an important albeit overlooked portion of an FSAE Racecar. Taking high velocity, low pressure flow and redirecting to low velocity high pressure flow allows for cooling of the radiator, and by extension the engine. Designed and analyzed in one academic quarter, using SolidWorks, ANSYS FLUENT and Star-CCM+. Manufactured using a MultiCam 3 axis router, and HSMWorks. Final parts were manufactured using dry layup molds and Torray T700 multiweave. Unfortunately due to the highly competitive nature of the competition, I have to remain publicly tight lipped about some aspects of the design.
